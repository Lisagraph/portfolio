While taking classes at uni and TAFE focusing on UI design, I started learning to code. I enjoy my work because I love art so consider myself fortunate to have been able to make it my profession. After work, I'm up for outdoor recreation.

On this page, I'll outline my experience working in roles as a digital graphic/UI designer, my art and design background and studies.
Click this link   Equitise   to see some mock-ups I've been working on to present for a home-page rejig, some web banners and an email design.

Some of my experience is drawing and painting. I started with watercolour mediums as a child and progressed to digital art during my studies. Now I'm drawing in Adobe illustrator and learning to draw in css and javascript. I used Illustrator to create the butterfly drawing pictured here. The dragonfly and locket are real watercolour on cotton parchment.
For Equitise, I draw flat avatar concepts, which are being using to update website graphics and digital media.

Equitise required that I learn to write code necessary for email and I was confident I could do it because I'd been writing javascript expressions in Adobe After Effects, i-frames in Adobe XD for some time. I learned the inline table technique to use in Autopilot and Mailchimp and quickly understood that I would need to learn to write complete javascript to design full webpages, so began the coding-for beginners-journey. This is year 2 of the journey and I have used the skills I've developed so far to write my Portfolio. I intend to continue to increase my coding skillset so as to be as effective as possible in my role.

Over the past few years, Ive been working between part time and contract positions to further my design knowledge and skillset. Right now, I'm learning javascript and efficient coding practice while working part time. My objective is to begin working in an additional role, where I can add value to the business by applying my growing design skillset.

Working as a digital graphic designer for Equitise is fun. I service the marketing team alongside Equitise's Co-founder Jonny Wilkninson. The team trust me to work consistently online. A typical day at Equitise sees us onboard clients and provide them with our marketing package to support their equity crowdfund campaign. We supply graphics for use across digital platforms and write emails and web banners to represent the client's campaign. Quite often the client has no marketing material as they are an early startup, in which case I've been known to get creative. We assist with their videos where I create intros, outros and provide general video and graphic editing services.

Working as a digital graphic designer for BuyNatural was a great role. The marketing team work closely with both of the Owners, Matt and Catherine, who are very involved with the business. BuyNatural onboarded many clients, which involved providing the client with their opted marketing package. My role entailed photography/videography, social media content production including drawing/graphic/video content and web banner design. I was able to get comfortable with animation and web design concept as the Owners encourage creativity and inventivness, especially for early stage clients who have little to no material. The Owners trusted me to start working online as it was a 2 hour drive each way and then the COVID19 lockdowns began so working online became essential to maintain productivity. I still help with the occasional contract for BuyNatural as they are a pleasure to work for.

Volunteer duties include assisting with IT requirements and teaching IT basics to Habitat volunteers and members to enable them to fulfill their roles. I provide photography, video and graphic design services when required for social media, promotional and event material.
